Top of the Mark — the “Squadron Bottle”

What it is: A sky lounge on the 19th floor of the Mark Hopkins atop Nob Hill — floor-to-ceiling windows, 360° views, a 1939 landmark.

The story: In WWII, Pacific-bound servicemen took their last drink here before shipping out. Wives and sweethearts watched the ships sail from these windows. The men started a tradition — the Squadron Bottle: a departing unit bought a bottle, each man took one drink and signed it, and it was left behind the bar for the next man to finish. The last survivor drank the final pour. A vow of brotherhood — “see you on the other side.”

Why it's yours: Two Marines, on the country's 250th, standing where Marines drank before war, taking that shot with the city below. Not a tourist stop — a pilgrimage.

Sunset. Collared shirt. Flash military IDs, ask about the tradition. 999 California St — ~12-min Waymo (uphill, don't walk it), ~$10–14.

Fireworks Playbook

Jul 4, ~9:30p, off the Golden Gate Bridge towers. Where to stand + the hacks.
The catch

They launch from the northwest (the bridge). You need a clear sightline toward the bridge. The Ferry Building, the Embarcadero, and Ocean Beach all face the wrong way — skip them.

Best spots, ranked
Crissy FieldThe pick. Wide open lawn facing the bridge, room to spread out. Get there 2–3 hrs early.★ best
Marina GreenCloser to the action, smaller crowd than Crissy. Strong backup.★ solid
Pier 39 / N. EmbarcaderoClosest to your hotel; the block by Jefferson & Taylor. Crowded but easy.closest
Twin PeaksWide skyline shot from 900 ft — smaller bursts, but no crowd and an easy drive-up/leave.photos
Hacks & secrets
  • Chesty's is the wrong way. The Marine club's 12th-floor Chesty's Bar faces downtown/Union Square, not the bridge — great for a drink, but you won't see these fireworks from it.
  • Beat the rideshare trap. Cars can't reach the waterfront after the show and surge hard. Get dropped a few blocks inland, walk in — and walk several blocks back out before you order the ride home.
  • The bridge closes. Fully shut to cars ~9–10p; US-101 approaches start closing 8p. Don't plan to drive near it.
  • Layer up. Karl the Fog rolls in cold and damp after sunset at the Golden Gate — bring a jacket even if the day was warm.
  • Arrive on the early side. Best lawn at Crissy/Marina fills 2–3 hrs before the 9:30p start. Sunset's ~8:35p, full dark ~9:15p.
  • Hidden add-on. A ticketed Pier 45 dockside party ($55–82) throws in a laser show off Alcatraz if you want a bundled night on the water.
  • No drones in SF. The Bay Area drone shows are all across the water (Mare Island, San Jose) — not worth leaving the city. The bridge is the show.
